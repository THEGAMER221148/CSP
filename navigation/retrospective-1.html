How I've Changed

Comparing myself now to the beginning of the year

Almost three months ago, I came into this class for the first time. I had taken CSSE last year, and had some computer science experience, but I had no experience the AP CSP course.
Before starting this course, I wasn't great at leading. I could be the leader of a group project, but would sometimes get lost or just "go with the flow." However, this class has taught me something valuable - how to take control and work as a team. Now, after going through nearly the entire first trimester, I have developed critical teamworking skills that will undoubtably benifit me in the future. I've also improved my awareness and communication skills, getting better at being "on top of things."

Sprints & Key Ideas

Remembering Things from Three Sprints

(I recognize that there's a lot of text here, so I've highlighted key words to make reading easier)

Throughout the year, we completed three sprints.

The first one focused on tool installation and a basic introduction of how CSP is taught. Since I took CSSE last year, I alreasy had some experience going into the tools installation and did not have to start from scratch. I already has WSL, Ubuntu, VSCode, and Python installed on my computer, and I had a basic understanding of the linux commands in the terminal. What I mainly learned from that first sprint was more about the Linux terminal, since that was something I needed a little more practice with at the time.

The second sprint was where things really picked up. It was mostly about Javascript and Python, and although I was already familiar with these programming languages, the topics we covered weren't all things I was experienced in. For instance, algorithmic efficiency was a relatively new topic, as I hadn't known about it for long before I had to build my lesson on it for the class. One of the student-led homeworks involving an API was also challenging as I had never done things like that before. However, most of the material covered was pretty basic stuff that I already had a good understanding of.

The third and final sprint of the trimester was my favorite one. In sprint 3, we mainly focused on putting our coding experience we gained in sprint 2 to use in the form of a space-themed questified learning experience called Digital Famine. It was here that we created interactive lessons on various computer science and web concepts that all came together to form a fun quest. Having created the lesson about databases, I definitely gained some experience with database security and encryption. We also incoorperated microblogging into our lessons in the form of a feedback discussion board, which was undoubtably a focus of the sprint.

My N@TM Experience

How Night at the Museum went this trimester!

During this trimester's Night at the Museum event, I presented my level of the class's quest-like learing experience Digital Famine along with my team. Our presentation went smoothly, and thanks to the microblogging feature, we got some nice comments telling us that we had useful information!

Another nice thing about N@TM is that I got an opportunity to look at what the other classes created, and safe to say it did not dissapoint! CSSE's West Coast project was definitely fun to play learn about.

Expanding Digital Famine

"What do you want to do next on project, given time?"

If we had more time to work on our section of Digital Famine, we would make the site easier to navigate and less complex so that those learning from the lesson could have an easier time navigating and understanding the material.

Next Steps in CompSci

"What do you want to learn next in CompSci?"

While there are many aspects of computer science that I would find interesting, the topics I look forward to learning are databases, encryption, and live communication. These topics interest me, mainly because I see them used in the real world and can think of so many applications in which they would be useful.
